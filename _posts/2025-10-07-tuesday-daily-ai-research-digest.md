### 1. Agentic Context Engineering: Evolving Contexts for Self-Improving
  Language Models

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.04618)

ACE, a framework for adaptive context engineering, enhances LLM applications by preserving detailed knowledge through structured updates, outperforming baselines in agent and domain-specific tasks with reduced adaptation costs.

### 2. Paper2Video: Automatic Video Generation from Scientific Papers

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.05096)

PaperTalker is a multi-agent framework that automates academic presentation video generation by integrating slide generation, layout refinement, subtitling, speech synthesis, and talking-head rendering, outperforming existing methods.

### 3. Video-LMM Post-Training: A Deep Dive into Video Reasoning with Large
  Multimodal Models

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.05034)

This survey examines post-training methodologies for Video-LMMs, focusing on supervised fine-tuning, reinforcement learning, and test-time scaling, while addressing challenges in video understanding.

### 4. MITS: Enhanced Tree Search Reasoning for LLMs via Pointwise Mutual
  Information

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.03632)

Mutual Information Tree Search (MITS) uses information-theoretic principles to guide and evaluate reasoning paths in large language models, improving performance and efficiency.

### 5. VChain: Chain-of-Visual-Thought for Reasoning in Video Generation

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.05094)

VChain enhances video generation by integrating visual reasoning from multimodal models to guide sparse tuning of a pre-trained video generator.

### 6. Hybrid Architectures for Language Models: Systematic Analysis and Design
  Insights

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.04800)

A comprehensive evaluation of hybrid language models combining self-attention with structured state space models, analyzing inter-layer and intra-layer fusion strategies, and providing design recommendations.

### 7. Imperceptible Jailbreaking against Large Language Models

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.05025)

Imperceptible jailbreaks using Unicode variation selectors enable high attack success rates against aligned LLMs without visible prompt modifications.

### 8. Optimal Scaling Needs Optimal Norm

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.03871)

Joint optimal scaling of model and dataset sizes in deep learning is governed by the operator norm of the output layer, a phenomenon termed norm transfer, which provides a necessary condition for optimal learning rate and batch size.

### 9. Reactive Transformer (RxT) -- Stateful Real-Time Processing for
  Event-Driven Reactive Language Models

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.03561)

The Reactive Transformer (RxT) addresses the limitations of stateless Transformers in conversational AI by using an event-driven paradigm with a fixed-size Short-Term Memory (STM) system, achieving linear scaling and low latency.

### 10. Front-Loading Reasoning: The Synergy between Pretraining and
  Post-Training Data

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2510.03264)

Introducing reasoning data during pretraining significantly enhances LLM performance compared to post-training, with pretraining benefiting more from diverse data patterns while SFT benefits more from high-quality data.
