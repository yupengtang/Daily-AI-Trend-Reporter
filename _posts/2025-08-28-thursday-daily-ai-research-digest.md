### 1. Beyond Transcription: Mechanistic Interpretability in ASR

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.15882)

Interpretability methods like logit lens, linear probing, and activation patching are applied to ASR to uncover internal dynamics, repetition hallucinations, and semantic biases, enhancing model transparency and robustness.

### 2. Self-Rewarding Vision-Language Model via Reasoning Decomposition

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.19652)

Vision-SR1 uses reinforcement learning to enhance visual reasoning in vision-language models by decomposing the process into visual perception and language reasoning stages, improving accuracy and reducing hallucinations.

### 3. CODA: Coordinating the Cerebrum and Cerebellum for a Dual-Brain Computer
  Use Agent with Decoupled Reinforcement Learning

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.20096)

CODA, a trainable compositional framework, combines a generalist planner and specialist executor to achieve robust execution and cross-domain generalization in scientific computing GUIs.

### 4. Analysing Chain of Thought Dynamics: Active Guidance or Unfaithful
  Post-hoc Rationalisation?

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.19827)

Investigation into Chain-of-Thought dynamics and faithfulness across various models reveals inconsistencies in their reliance on CoT and its alignment with actual reasoning.

### 5. Discrete Diffusion VLA: Bringing Discrete Diffusion to Action Decoding
  in Vision-Language-Action Policies

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.20072)

Discrete Diffusion VLA uses a single-transformer policy with discrete diffusion to model actions, improving decoding order, consistency, and performance over autoregressive and continuous diffusion methods.

### 6. MIDAS: Multimodal Interactive Digital-human Synthesis via Real-time
  Autoregressive Video Generation

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.19320)

An autoregressive video generation framework with multimodal control and low-latency extrapolation uses a modified large language model and a deep compression autoencoder to achieve high efficiency and fine-grained controllability.

### 7. Diffusion Language Models Know the Answer Before Decoding

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.19982)

Prophet, a training-free fast decoding paradigm for diffusion language models, reduces inference time by leveraging early answer convergence without sacrificing quality.

### 8. Predicting the Order of Upcoming Tokens Improves Language Modeling

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.19228)

Token Order Prediction (TOP) improves language model training by ordering upcoming tokens, outperforming both Next-Token Prediction (NTP) and Multi-Token Prediction (MTP) across benchmarks.

### 9. AudioStory: Generating Long-Form Narrative Audio with Large Language
  Models

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.20088)

AudioStory integrates large language models with text-to-audio systems to generate coherent, long-form audio narratives through a unified framework with decoupled bridging mechanisms and end-to-end training.

### 10. StepWiser: Stepwise Generative Judges for Wiser Reasoning

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2508.19229)

A generative judge model, StepWiser, uses reinforcement learning to provide step-by-step reasoning feedback, improving both training and inference performance of policy models.
