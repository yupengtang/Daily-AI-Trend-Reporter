### 1. The Dragon Hatchling: The Missing Link between the Transformer and
  Models of the Brain

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.26507)

BDH, a biologically inspired Large Language Model, combines scale-free network architecture with Hebbian learning to achieve Transformer-like performance while maintaining interpretability.

### 2. MCPMark: A Benchmark for Stress-Testing Realistic and Comprehensive MCP
  Use

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.24002)

MCPMark is a comprehensive benchmark for evaluating MCP use in real-world workflows, featuring diverse tasks that require richer interactions with the environment, and reveals that current LLMs perform poorly on these tasks.

### 3. Vision-Zero: Scalable VLM Self-Improvement via Strategic Gamified
  Self-Play

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.25541)

Vision-Zero is a domain-agnostic framework that enhances vision-language models through self-improvement in competitive visual games, using Iterative Self-Play Policy Optimization and achieving state-of-the-art performance without human annotation.

### 4. More Thought, Less Accuracy? On the Dual Nature of Reasoning in
  Vision-Language Models

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.25848)

VAPO-Thinker-7B enhances multimodal reasoning by anchoring the process to visual information, improving performance on visual tasks while maintaining logical inference.

### 5. Winning the Pruning Gamble: A Unified Approach to Joint Sample and Token
  Pruning for Efficient Supervised Fine-Tuning

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.23873)

Quadrant-based Tuning (Q-Tuning) optimizes both sample and token pruning in supervised fine-tuning of large language models, achieving superior performance with reduced data.

### 6. TruthRL: Incentivizing Truthful LLMs via Reinforcement Learning

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.25760)

TruthRL, a reinforcement learning framework, enhances the truthfulness of large language models by balancing accuracy and abstention, significantly reducing hallucinations and improving performance across benchmarks.

### 7. Learning to See Before Seeing: Demystifying LLM Visual Priors from
  Language Pre-training

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.26625)

LLMs develop visual priors during language pre-training, which can be leveraged for vision tasks with minimal additional data, and these priors are composed of separable perception and reasoning components.

### 8. DC-VideoGen: Efficient Video Generation with Deep Compression Video
  Autoencoder

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.25182)

DC-VideoGen accelerates video generation by adapting pre-trained diffusion models to a deep compression latent space, reducing inference latency and enabling high-resolution video generation.

### 9. OceanGym: A Benchmark Environment for Underwater Embodied Agents

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.26536)

OceanGym is a benchmark for underwater embodied agents using Multi-modal Large Language Models to address challenges in perception, planning, and adaptability in harsh ocean environments.

### 10. Thinking-Free Policy Initialization Makes Distilled Reasoning Models
  More Effective and Efficient Reasoners

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2509.26226)

TFPI, a simple adaptation to RLVR, improves performance and reduces token usage by discarding thinking content during training, accelerating RL convergence and achieving higher accuracy with less computational cost.
