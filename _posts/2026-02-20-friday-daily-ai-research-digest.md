### 2. Mobile-Agent-v3.5: Multi-platform Fundamental GUI Agents

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.16855)

GUI-Owl-1.5 is a multi-platform GUI agent model achieving state-of-the-art performance on GUI automation, grounding, tool-calling, and memory tasks through hybrid data pipelines, unified reasoning enhancement, and multi-platform reinforcement learning.

### 3. SpargeAttention2: Trainable Sparse Attention via Hybrid Top-k+Top-p Masking and Distillation Fine-Tuning

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.13515)

A trainable sparse attention method called SpargeAttention2 is proposed that achieves high sparsity in diffusion models while maintaining generation quality through hybrid masking rules and distillation-inspired fine-tuning.

### 6. Computer-Using World Model

### 7. Discovering Multiagent Learning Algorithms with Large Language Models

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.16928)

AlphaEvolve, an evolutionary coding agent using large language models, automatically discovers new multiagent learning algorithms for imperfect-information games by evolving regret minimization and population-based training variants.

### 8. Calibrate-Then-Act: Cost-Aware Exploration in LLM Agents

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.16699)

Large language models can be improved for complex tasks by explicitly reasoning about cost-uncertainty tradeoffs through a Calibrate-Then-Act framework that enhances decision-making in sequential environments.

### 9. "What Are You Doing?": Effects of Intermediate Feedback from Agentic LLM In-Car Assistants During Multi-Step Processing

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.15569)

Users prefer adaptive feedback mechanisms in in-car AI assistants, starting with high transparency to build trust and then reducing verbosity as reliability increases, particularly in attention-critical driving scenarios.

### 10. DDiT: Dynamic Patch Scheduling for Efficient Diffusion Transformers

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.16968)

Dynamic tokenization improves diffusion transformer efficiency by adjusting patch sizes based on content complexity and denoising timestep, achieving significant speedup without quality loss.
