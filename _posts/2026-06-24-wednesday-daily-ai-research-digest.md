### 1. Qwen-AgentWorld: Language World Models for General Agents

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.24597)

Language-based world models enable agentic environment simulation across multiple domains and enhance general agent performance through scalable simulation and improved downstream task performance.

### 2. NatureBench: Can Coding Agents Match the Published SOTA of Nature-Family Papers?

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.24530)

NatureBench presents a cross-disciplinary benchmark of 90 scientific tasks derived from Nature publications to assess AI coding agents' ability to achieve discovery rather than just reproduction, revealing that current agents primarily rely on methodological translation rather than genuine scientific innovation.

### 3. MobileForge: Annotation-Free Adaptation for Mobile GUI Agents with Hierarchical Feedback-Guided Policy Optimization

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.19930)

MobileForge enables efficient adaptation of mobile GUI agents through annotation-free learning by combining real app interaction grounding with hierarchical feedback-guided policy optimization.

### 4. MemGUI-Agent: An End-to-End Long-Horizon Mobile GUI Agent with Proactive Context Management

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.19926)

MemGUI-Agent addresses long-horizon mobile GUI task limitations through proactive context management using Context-as-Action (ConAct) to maintain critical information across extended sequences.

### 5. AOHP: An Open-Source OS-Level Agent Harness for Personalized, Efficient and Secure Interaction

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.23449)

AOHP presents an Android-based operating system framework that treats AI agents as first-class entities, enhancing task completion rates and reducing execution costs through specialized agent-oriented mechanisms.

### 6. LingxiDiagBench: A Multi-Agent Framework for Benchmarking LLMs in Chinese Psychiatric Consultation and Diagnosis

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2602.09379)

A large-scale multi-agent benchmark for evaluating LLMs in Chinese psychiatric diagnosis is introduced, highlighting challenges in dynamic consultation and the gap between consultation quality and diagnostic accuracy.

### 7. OpenThoughts-Agent: Data Recipes for Agentic Models

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.24855)

An open-source data curation pipeline for training agentic language models is presented, demonstrating superior performance through systematic experimentation and scalable training data.

### 8. Semantic Browsing: Controllable Diversity for Image Generation

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.23679)

Text-to-image models are enhanced with controlled diversity through semantic browsing capabilities that enable structured navigation of image variations based on meaningful semantic decisions.

### 9. FLAT: Feedforward Latent Triangle Splatting for Geometrically Accurate Scene Generation

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.24876)

Video diffusion models are adapted to decode explicit surface primitives directly from latent space, enabling high-quality 3D scene generation with improved geometric accuracy and real-time rendering capabilities.

### 10. FedOT: Ownership Verification and Leakage Tracing via Watermarks for Federated LDMs

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2606.22875)

FedOT is a novel framework that enables ownership verification and leakage tracing in federated latent diffusion models by introducing chunked watermarking and latent vector transformation to prevent watermark removal attacks.
