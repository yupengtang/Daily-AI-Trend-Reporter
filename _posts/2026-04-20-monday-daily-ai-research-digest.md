### 1. Elucidating the SNR-t Bias of Diffusion Probabilistic Models

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.16044)

Diffusion probabilistic models suffer from SNR-timestep bias during inference, which is addressed through a differential correction method that processes frequency components separately, improving generation quality across multiple models with minimal computational cost.

### 2. DiPO: Disentangled Perplexity Policy Optimization for Fine-grained Exploration-Exploitation Trade-Off

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.13902)

A novel reinforcement learning approach for large language models that addresses the exploration-exploitation trade-off through perplexity-based sample partitioning and bidirectional reward allocation mechanisms.

### 3. Qwen3.5-Omni Technical Report

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.15804)

Qwen3.5-Omni is a large-scale multimodal model with hundreds of billions of parameters that excels in audio-visual understanding and generation, featuring advanced architectures and novel capabilities like Audio-Visual Vibe Coding.

### 4. Maximal Brain Damage Without Data or Optimization: Disrupting Neural Networks via Sign-Bit Flips

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2502.07408)

Deep neural networks exhibit catastrophic vulnerability to minimal parameter bit flips across multiple domains, which can be identified and mitigated through targeted protection strategies.

### 5. PersonaVLM: Long-Term Personalized Multimodal LLMs

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.13074)

A novel personalized multimodal language model framework called PersonaVLM is introduced that enables long-term personalization through memory retention, multi-turn reasoning, and response alignment capabilities.

### 6. Web Retrieval-Aware Chunking (W-RAC) for Efficient and Cost-Effective Retrieval-Augmented Generation Systems

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.04936)

Web Retrieval-Aware Chunking (W-RAC) introduces a cost-efficient framework for web document processing that reduces LLM token usage and hallucination risks through structured content representation and retrieval-aware grouping decisions.

### 7. Cut Your Losses! Learning to Prune Paths Early for Efficient Parallel Reasoning

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.16029)

STOP is a systematic path pruning method for large reasoning models that improves efficiency and accuracy through learnable token-level pruning across different compute budgets.

### 8. Mind DeepResearch Technical Report

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.14518)

MindDR is an efficient multi-agent deep research framework that achieves high performance through a collaborative three-agent architecture and specialized four-stage training pipeline, demonstrating strong results on multiple benchmarks.

### 9. Where does output diversity collapse in post-training?

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.16027)

Output diversity collapse in post-trained language models is primarily driven by training data composition rather than generation format, with different post-training methods affecting diversity differently across tasks.

### 10. Motif-Video 2B: Technical Report

[Read Paper](https://huggingface.co/papers/2604.16503)

Motif-Video 2B achieves high text-to-video generation quality using a specialized architecture with shared cross-attention and three-part backbone, along with efficient training methods, while requiring significantly fewer parameters and training data than larger models.
